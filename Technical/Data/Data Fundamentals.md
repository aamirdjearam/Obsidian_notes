What is Data: "collection of facts, numbers, words or observations that can be processed and analyzed to extract meaning and make decisions"

Types of Data:
- Quantitative Data - Values that can be measured numerically
- Qualitative Data - descriptive and non-numerical
- Structured Data - Clear and Defined, often stored in [[Relational Databases]]
- Unstructured Data - lacks a strictly defined format, often comes in complex forms such as text docs, images, and videos. Often use [[Natural Language Processing]] to analyze unstructured data 
- Semi-Structured Data - Blend of structured and unstructured data. Examples include [[XML (Extensible Markup Language)|XML Files]] and [[JSON (JavaScript Object Notation)|JSON Objects]]
- Metadata - data about data, such as names, creation dates, etc.
- Big Data - massive complex data sets

Why is Data Important:
- Help drive innovation and growth
- Analyze, improve and predict customer related-actions

How is Data used:
- Predictive Analytics
- Generative AI
- Healthcare Innovations
- Social Science Research
- Cybersecurity and Risk Management
- Operational Efficiency
- Government Initiatives
- Business Intelligence (BI)
